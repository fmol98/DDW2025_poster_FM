Decreased CD8A and CD8B expressing blood T cell populations may predict adalimumab and infliximab response failure in Crohn’s disease patients.

Background

Mainstream biological treatment for Crohn's disease (CD) anti-tumor necrosis factor (anti-TNF) therapies, adalimumab and infliximab, are effective in inducing and maintaining remission in patients with Crohn’s disease. Nonetheless, up to 40% of CD patients fail to respond, emphasizing the need to characterize the molecular differences predicting response failure.

Aim

Assess predictive biomarkers for treatment efficacy

Methods

As part of the EPIC-CD cohort, we assembled a cohort of 57 adult CD patients that were scheduled to start adalimumab or infliximab treatment. Response to treatment was assessed after 6-9 months based on strict combination of endoscopic (≥50% reduction SES-CD), biochemical (CRP reduction ≥50% or CRP≤5 mg/L and FCP reduction ≥50% or FCP ≤250 µg/g) and clinical criteria (≥3 point drop in HBI or HBI ≤4 without corticosteroids). Whole peripheral blood leukocytes (PBL) were collected from patients prior to the start of treatment and at response assessment. Messenger RNA was extracted, converted into cDNA and sequenced. Differential expression (DE) analysis was carried out within the R statistical environment using the DESeq2 package (v 1.40.2), and cellular deconvolution using the granulator package (v 1.12.0). From the same patients, genomic DNA was extracted, bisulfite converted whereafter the DNA methylome was quantified using the Illumina HumanMethylationEPIC BeadChip array (v1). Differential methylation analyses were performed using the limma package (v 3.60.6), followed by estimation of blood cell distribution with the FlowSorted.Blood.EPIC package (v 2.8.0).

Results

Differential gene expression analysis identified a significantly (padj<0.05) higher expression of CD8A and CD8B for both adalimumab and infliximab responding patients prior to treatment. Concordantly, transcriptional pathways associated with cytotoxic activity, such as antiviral response and interferon response, were enriched in responders to anti TNF prior to treatment. Cell type deconvolution based on gene expression indeed suggested higher CD8T cell proportions in responders. Indeed, when interrogating the cellular deconvolution based on paired DNA methylome measurements corroborated the observations.

Conclusions

Our data suggest that non-response to anti-TNF can be associated with lower CD8T populations among relative to responders. This observation aligns with emerging evidence that underscores the heterogeneity and prognostic potential of CD8T subsets in inflammatory bowel disease (Lee, J.C., et al. 2011, JCI; Garduño & Däbritz, 2021, Front. Immunol.). Further research is needed to validate our observations and elucidate the functional implications of the larger CD8T population, which could lead to more personalized therapeutic approaches and improved treatment outcomes for CD patients.
